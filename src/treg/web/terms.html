03Teams, roles, and who controls what

Every account gets a personal team, and you can create or join others. Within a team:

  • Owners and admins can add and remove credentials and tools, invite and remove members, set each member's tool access and daily call cap, and delete the team and everything in it.
  • Members can call the tools they've been granted and read the team's skills. They never see raw secret values — the Service is built so a stored credential is never returned to a client.
  • Every call is logged — who, which tool, which method and path, the status code, and when. Team admins can see that log. If that isn't acceptable to you, don't call through a team you don't control.

If you join a team you don't own, the team's owner effectively controls that workspace: they can revoke your access, see your call history in it, and delete shared content. Choose your teams accordingly.

04Credentials, and what we do with them

The point of the Service is holding credentials so you don't have to hand them out. Concretely:

  • Secret values are encrypted at rest and are never returned through the API or shown in the dashboard after they're stored.
  • We inject them only into calls that an authenticated member of the owning team makes to a tool they have access to — through the call proxy, a local-run grant, or a server-side run.
  • We do not read, mine, sell, or use your credentials or the data they reach for any purpose other than performing the call you asked for and keeping the credential healthy (periodic validity checks and OAuth token refresh).

Your obligations. You must have the right to store and use every credential you add, and the authority to grant your teammates and agents access through it. You are responsible for the calls made with your credentials — including calls made by AI agents you've pointed at the Service. An agent acting under your token is you, as far as these Terms are concerned.

Treat treg as a working vault, not a system of record. Keep your own copy of any credential you can't re-issue, and revoke upstream if you ever suspect compromise — revoking at the source is always faster and more complete than anything we can do for you.
