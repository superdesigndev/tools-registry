Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026 Effective: 22 July 2026

The short version

  • We collect almost nothing about you. An email address to sign in, the teams you're in, and a log of which tools were called. That's the account.
  • Your credentials are encrypted and never returned. No stored secret value is ever sent back to a client, shown in the dashboard, or read by us.
  • We don't store what flows through the proxy. A call's request and response bodies pass through and are gone. We keep the method, path, status code, and timestamp — not the content.
  • No trackers. No analytics scripts, no advertising pixels, no third-party cookies. One session cookie, and that's it.
  • No training, no selling. Your credentials, skills, and connected-account data are never sold and never used to train models.

01Who this covers

This policy explains how Superdesign ("we", "us") handles personal data in the hosted tools-registry ("treg") service at treg.superdesign.dev. For that service, we are the data controller.

Self-hosted instances are not covered. If you or your employer run treg on your own infrastructure, your data never reaches us and this policy doesn't apply — the operator of that instance is the controller.

Contact for anything privacy-related: jason@superdesign.dev.

02What we collect

Account and identity

  • Your email address — the only identifier we require. Used to sign you in, send one-time codes and team invitations, and attribute actions in the audit log.
  • Team names, your role in each team, and when you joined.
  • Token hashes. We store a SHA-256 hash of each access token, never the token itself.
  • Short-lived sign-in codes and rate-limit counters, which expire automatically.

We do not ask for your name, phone number, address, photo, date of birth, or payment details — the Service is free and we take no payment data at all.

Credentials and connections

  • Secret values you store (API keys, tokens, credential files), encrypted at rest with authenticated encryption. These are never returned through the API and never displayed after they're saved.
  • OAuth tokens from accounts you connect, plus the metadata needed to use them: the provider, the scopes you actually granted, the specific property or account you selected and its display name, and expiry/refresh timestamps.
  • Health status for each credential ("does this key still work"), from periodic validity checks.

Usage and audit records

  • For each proxied or local-run call: the caller's email, the tool name, HTTP method, request path, status code, and timestamp.
  • For each server-side CLI run: the caller's email, the tool name, the command arguments, exit code, and duration. Secret values are injected as environment variables, never as command-line arguments, so they don't appear here.

We do not record request or response bodies. The data you fetch from an upstream API passes through the proxy and is not written down. What survives a call is the metadata listed above — enough for a team admin to see who used what, and no more.

Content you create

Skills (SKILL.md text and companion files), tool definitions, and their upstream URLs. Treat skill text as visible to everyone in the team it lives in — don't put secrets there.

Technical data

Our hosting provider processes standard server logs (IP address, user agent, request line, timestamp) for security and abuse prevention, retained on their default schedule. We don't build profiles from them.

04What we never do

  • We never sell personal data, credentials, or anything reached through them.
  • We never use your credentials, connected-account data, or skill content to train machine learning models — ours or anyone else's.
  • We never run advertising, analytics, or tracking scripts on our pages. There is no Google Analytics, no pixel, no session-replay, no third-party cookie.
  • We never use your stored credentials for anything other than the call you or a permitted teammate initiated, plus automated health checks and token refreshes.

05Google user data — Limited Use

When you connect Google Search Console, Google Analytics, Google Business Profile, or Google Ads, treg's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically:

  • Google user data is used only to provide and improve the features you connected it for — making the API calls you or your team initiate.
  • It is not transferred to others except as necessary to provide those features, to comply with applicable law, or as part of a merger or acquisition with notice to you.
  • It is not used for advertising, and is not used to train generalized AI or ML models.
  • No human at Superdesign reads your Google user data, except with your explicit consent for a specific support issue, for security purposes, to comply with applicable law, or where the data is aggregated and anonymized.
  • Disconnecting the provider in treg deletes the stored token. You can also revoke treg's access at any time from your Google account permissions page.

The same commitments apply to every other provider you connect (LinkedIn, Slack, X, or an API you register yourself).

07Cookies

One cookie: treg_session. It's HTTP-only, SameSite=Lax, and secure over HTTPS. It holds a signed session reference and nothing else. It's strictly necessary to keep you signed in, so there's no consent banner and nothing to opt out of short of signing out.

There are no analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking cookies anywhere on the Service.

08How we protect it

  • Secret values and OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest with a key held outside the database.
  • Access tokens are stored only as SHA-256 hashes; a database leak doesn't yield usable tokens.
  • All traffic is over HTTPS. Every record is scoped to an organization, and access is checked per request against your membership, role, and per-member tool list.
  • Server-side runs execute in a sandbox with filesystem and network egress restrictions.
  • Bumping your token version revokes every token you hold at once, immediately.

No system is perfectly secure. If we become aware of a breach affecting your personal data, we'll notify affected users and any required regulator without undue delay.

10Your rights

Wherever you live, you can ask us to access, correct, export, or delete your data, and to restrict or object to processing. Depending on your location (EEA/UK under the GDPR, California under the CCPA/CPRA, and similar laws elsewhere) some of these are formal legal rights; we honour all of them for everyone regardless.

  • Self-serve: the dashboard lets you delete credentials, tools, skills, and whole teams, and revoke all your tokens.
  • Everything else: email jason@superdesign.dev. We'll respond within 30 days and won't charge you or treat you differently for asking.

California residents: we do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined by the CPRA, and we have not done so in the preceding 12 months.

EEA/UK residents: you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. Data is processed in the United States; where a transfer mechanism is required, we rely on the Standard Contractual Clauses offered by our processors.

11Children

The Service isn't intended for anyone under 16, and we don't knowingly collect their data. If you believe a child has created an account, email us and we'll delete it.
